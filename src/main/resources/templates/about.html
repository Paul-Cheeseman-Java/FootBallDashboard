About:

I enjoy both software development (I am looking to forge a new career in this area) and football, and I have always been amazed at the state of the transfer market and the amount of money flowing through it. Every year it seems to get more crazy! So it seemed a good choice of subject to explore these new technologies with.

The site has been created to show my ability in website development and specifically, DC.js and the supporting software D3.js and Crossfilter, in addition to Flask, which is a sever side web mirco-framework that this site runs off.

The data for the site is based on data from the website Transfer League which has various stats on associated with Premier and Championship transfers.

Transfer league is a static website with a detailed view. The aim of this site was to take a broader view of the Premier League and Championship transfer data and present an interactive interface where users could drill down into the data. In essence I wanted to use some of the data but present a different and interactive perspective.

There are slight differences in the data between Transfer League and this site, in that 'undisclosed fees' are counted as 0 on this site, so consider the totals shown on this site, an 'at least' value, rather than an attempt to get an exact value.
